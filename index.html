Hi, I'm Evan

I'm a bodyworker and artist. My work begins with listening: through touch, attention, and time. I'm drawn to how the body reorganizes itself when it feels safe enough to let go of what it's been gripping.

Through receptive touch and unhurried, consistent meetings, something settles. The body already knows how to hold itself; my job is to help it trust that again.

My mother was a woodworker and my father a painter. From them I learned how form and presence shape each other, how what's held and what holds are always in dialogue. My art and bodywork come from the same ground, exploring the rhythms between body and space, inhabitant and environment.

Whether through contact or creation, my work makes room for noticing, for release, for the quiet intelligence that's already at work in us.

What My Bodywork Session Are Like

In many ways this modality is like a dance, but you're relaxed on the table. I'm gently leading parts of your body in safe, but unexpected directions and you get to practice surrendering into a fluid motion. It can be as simple as me moving your shoulder around just a few inches at a time. Together these little movements become a slow repetition of micro trust falls.

I am also listening (through touch) for edges where protection begins and waiting until your system decides it’s safe to soften. We dance on these edges until they expand. Our aligned attention allows space to open and holding patterns to let go.

During the session, what people notice most is being met, really listened to. Afterward, something settles. A tiredness they didn't know was there comes to the surface. The body stops holding itself together so tightly. One client described it as feeling herself fall into alignment.

How Being Met Is Different From Being Worked On

  • Most tension isn’t mechanical, it’s protective
  • Your attention is required in the beginning of the session
  • Once trust is built, your attention can relax into ease
  • This trust helps retrain the protective holding patterns your body uses to stay safe

This Is Not Swedish Massage

  • I'll occasionally use a bit of effleurage, but there's no lotion or oil involved
  • Recommended clothing is thin and loose-fitting; wear whatever feels most comfortable to you
  • Swedish massage focuses on muscle tissue; I work on joints, attachment points, and somatic holding patterns

This Is Good For Those Who:

  • You need to feel safe before you can relax
  • You've tried massage but it doesn't 'stick'
  • You hold tension in your jaw, shoulders, or hips that feels wired to your nervous system
  • Want deep pressure, but only when approached gradually
