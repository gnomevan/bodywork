Touch Is a Conversation

Consent is not permission granted or withheld.

It's the living recognition that happens when two fields meet, acknowledge each others sovereignty and say: "I see you seeing me. We are here together. What we create will be ours."

Hi, I'm Evan

I’m a bodyworker, an artist, and a sensitive listener. My obsession with understanding how everything works led me eventually to our nervous system. The magical treelike structure that runs through our bodies and somehow reaches beyond is our connection to the world. We fixate on the 5 primary senses like they are doorways, but that is an oversimplified story. I prefer to see us as nexus points embedded in a web. The 4 sense organs on our head are specialists in specific frequencies. Touch is a sacred gateway where 2 bodies have direct access to the feedback loops that unite us.

Through this lens I listen to spaces and materials to make art out of patterns and I listen to bodies to allow them to release the protective patterns they've been holding.

What Happens in a session

In many ways it's like a dance where I'm gently leading your body in unexpected, but safe ways and you get to practice surrendering into a fluid motion. This practice of tiny moments of surrender is where the healing happens. It will be as simple as me moving your shoulder around just a few inches at a time.

Together these movements become a slow repetition of micro trust falls. I am also listening(through touch) for edges where protection begins and waiting until your system decides it’s safe to soften. We dance on these edges until they expand. Our aligned attention allows space to open and holding patterns to let go.

Being Met, Not Worked On

  • Most tension isn’t mechanical. It’s protective.
  • Your attention is required in the beggining of the session
  • Once trust is built your attention can relax into ease
  • Trust retrains the holding patterns that the body uses to stay safe

This is not sweedish massage

  • Sweedish works by freeing stagnent fluid (blood and lymph), bringing fresh blood to muscles
  • It feels great and is very beneficial for health and relaxation
  • It doesn't treat deeper holding patterns, which is where I stay focused
  • This technique works best with comfortable loose fitting clothing, or however you feel most relaxed

Who This Is For

This work may be a good fit if you:

  • Require attunment to feel safe
  • Understand how the body releases tension or want to
  • Want slowness instead of force
  • Feel sensitive or easily overwhelmed
  • Desire some deep pressure, but only when it is worked up to
